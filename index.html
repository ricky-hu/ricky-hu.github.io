I am a Master of Applied Science student at the University of British Columbia (UBC) in the departments of Electrical and Computer Engineering and Biomedical Engineering. My research is in developing quantifiable detection of pregnancy disorders with elastography and ultrasound processing techniques. I have a strong interest in integrating applied math and engineering in clinical settings to solve complex challenges in healthcare, working closely with research engineers, sonographers, radiologists, pathologists, and surgeons.

I graduated from the Engineering Physics program at UBC, specializing in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Honours Mathematics. I have experience a variety of engineering skills including programming, 3D modelling/printing, circuit design, machining, mathematical modelling, and robotics. I am ethusiastically invovling myself in healthcare and medicine through further coursework and collaboration in life sciences.

Outside of school and work for over ten years, I am an avid sailing instructor, water rescue team member, scout leader, table tennis player, and hockey fan. Feel free to browse and learn more about or contact me!

I am a Master of Applied Science student at the University of British Columbia (UBC), working in the Robotics and Control Laboratory. My research is in developing quantifiable detection of pregnancy disorders with elastography and ultrasound processing techniques, working closely with research engineers, sonographers, radiologists, pathologists, and surgeons.

I graduated from the Engineering Physics program at UBC in 2016, specializing in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a minor in Honours Mathematics. Some of my skills include programming, 3D modelling/printing, circuit design, machining, mathematical modelling, and robotics. I am developing a strong focus in healthcare through further coursework and collaboration with the UBC Faculty of Medicine.

Outside of school and work for over ten years, I am an avid sailing instructor, water rescue team member, scout leader, dodgeball player, and hockey fan. Feel free to browse and learn more about me!
